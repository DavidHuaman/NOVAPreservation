Northern Virginia Affordable Housing Preservation Study

Before you explore the map

This map shows subsidized affordable housing across nine Northern Virginia jurisdictions and flags which properties are most at risk of being lost to redevelopment or converted to market rate. The study's central argument is that who owns a property is the strongest predictor of whether it stays affordable, for-profit owners can sell or reposition, while mission-driven nonprofits, housing authorities, and public-private partnerships rarely do. Virginia's new Right of First Refusal law (HB 4) and the LIHTC Year-15 exit window, is the backbone of how risk is assigned here. To explore it, color properties by ownership, risk, or ROFR status, filter by jurisdiction, and click any property for details, where each circle is a property and its size reflects unit count. Data was pulled from NHPD and HUD data.
